Hi, I am Fujael, a Digital Humanities graduate student at the University of Alberta. I come from a background in Information Science and Library Management, which is where my curiosity about how knowledge is organized and accessed first took shape.

These days, I spend a lot of time making interactive stories in Twine, exploring networks analysis, and poking at data to see what it hides. I am genuinely fascinated by electronic literature. The idea that a game or a story can be an argument, that the 
path you take through something changes what it means.

At the core of it, I care about how technology and culture shape the way we share and experience knowledge, and whether the systems we build actually work for everyone.
